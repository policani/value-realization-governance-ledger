# Review Cadence Rules

## Cadence by benefit maturity

| Stage | Suggested review |
|---|---|
| Pre-baseline | One-time cleanup before ledger entry is trusted. |
| In delivery | Monthly or milestone-based, focused on measure readiness and owner confirmation. |
| Early adoption | Biweekly or monthly, focused on usage, operational signal, and leading indicators. |
| Benefit window open | Monthly or quarterly, focused on actual outcome movement. |
| Stabilized benefit | Quarterly, until accepted, retired, or moved to operational KPI ownership. |
| Disputed or weak evidence | Review at next governance meeting with explicit evidence owner action. |

## Aging logic

- Evidence older than one review cycle should be flagged as stale.
- A missing baseline should not remain unresolved beyond the first benefits review.
- A high-risk benefit should not roll forward without an owner action.
- A scale recommendation should require enough evidence to show repeatability, not just one positive datapoint.
