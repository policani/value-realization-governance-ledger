# Operating Model

## Purpose

The Value Realization Governance Ledger preserves the thread between approved intent and measured outcome. It helps leaders see what benefits were promised, what evidence exists, what remains uncertain, who owns the measure, and what human review decision is needed next.

## Starts when

An initiative has an approved business case, project charter, portfolio score, or equivalent governance record that states expected value.

## Ends when

A value-realization ledger and benefits review summary are ready for sponsor, PMO, or executive discussion.

## Owns

- Benefit claim normalization.
- Benefit hypothesis framing.
- Metric structure.
- Baseline / target / actual fields.
- Measure owner and source capture.
- Evidence confidence review.
- Realization risk review.
- Review cadence recommendation.
- Scale / continue / hold / retire recommendation for human review.

## Does not own

- Business case approval.
- Charter development.
- Portfolio scoring or prioritization.
- Official finance calculations.
- Savings certification.
- Funding, continuation, retirement, or scaling decisions.
- Live-system updates.

## Core principle

A weakly evidenced benefit is not a failed benefit. It is a governance signal that requires better evidence, better ownership, a different metric, or a human decision.
