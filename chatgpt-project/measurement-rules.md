# Measurement Rules

## Measurement contract

Every benefit should answer:

1. What outcome was expected?
2. What benefit type is being claimed?
3. What metric proves or disproves progress?
4. What was the baseline?
5. What target was approved?
6. What actual result is available?
7. What measurement period does the result cover?
8. What source produced the measurement?
9. Who owns the measure?
10. How often should it be reviewed?
11. What validation is still required?
12. What confidence level should be applied?
13. What realization risk remains?
14. Is the claim finance-sensitive?
15. What downstream route should receive follow-up?

## Baseline rules

- Use the pre-change value whenever possible.
- If no baseline exists, mark it as missing and recommend reconstruction only if the human owner approves the method.
- Do not invent historical baselines.

## Target rules

- Keep approved targets separate from later forecasts.
- If the target changed, show both the original target and revised target with owner confirmation.

## Actual rules

- State the period measured.
- State the source.
- Distinguish observed result from estimated result.
- Flag self-reported actuals as lower confidence unless corroborated.

## Financial rules

Financial benefits require finance-approved methods before they become official. Until then, label them as estimates, directional indicators, or pending finance confirmation.

## Downstream route rules

- Route owner actions, missing baselines, missing actuals, stale sources, validation requests, or high realization risk to PMO Governance Operations Log.
- Route executive attention items to Executive Portfolio Review Pack Builder.
- Keep ledger-only rows in this module when no action or executive decision is needed.
- Do not route unsupported financial claims as certified ROI.
